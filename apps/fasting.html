Fasting Tracker — IF, Ketosis & Autophagy

Widget Health Nutrition

Fasting Tracker is a Garmin Connect IQ intermittent fasting app for compatible Garmin watches. It helps you track fasting windows — including 16:8, 18:6, 20:4, OMAD, and custom fasting goals — directly from your wrist. No phone required. Your fasting data stays on your watch.

The intermittent fasting tracker built for your wrist. Watch your estimated fasting phase advance in real time — from Fed State to Fat Burning, Ketosis, and Autophagy — as the progress ring fills toward your fasting goal.

Features

  • Live phase tracker: Fed State → Fat Burning → Ketosis → Autophagy → Peak Autophagy
  • Animated progress arc — fills second by second as your fast advances
  • Countdown to the next metabolic milestone
  • 7-day fasting history chart — see your consistency at a glance
  • Lifetime stats: total fasts, longest fast, average duration, streak
  • Glance view — check your fasting status without opening the app
  • Flexible plans: 16:8, 18:6, 20:4, OMAD, or any custom fasting target
  • Forgot to stop? Adjust start time retroactively — up to 16 hours back
  • Vibration alert the moment your fasting goal is reached
  • Runs fully offline — your data never leaves your watch

Estimated fasting phases

  • Fed State (0h) — digestion active
  • Digesting (4h) — processing slows
  • Fat Burning (8h) — glycogen depleting
  • Crossover (12h) — shifting to fat as primary fuel
  • Ketosis (14h) — ketone production begins
  • Autophagy (20h) — cellular recycling phase
  • Peak Autophagy (24h+) — extended fasting range

Fasting phases are estimates based on elapsed fasting time and general nutritional research. They are for informational and wellness tracking purposes only and are not medical advice. Individual metabolic responses vary significantly. Consult a qualified healthcare professional before starting or changing any fasting protocol.

How to install Fasting Tracker on your Garmin watch

  1. Open the Garmin Connect IQ Store listing for Fasting Tracker
  2. Tap Download (or Try for the 48-hour free trial)
  3. Open the Garmin Connect app on your phone and sync your watch
  4. Find Fasting Tracker in your watch's widget or glance loop
  5. Set your fasting plan in Garmin Connect → My Apps → Fasting Tracker → Settings

Frequently asked questions

Can Garmin track intermittent fasting?

Garmin watches do not include a native intermittent fasting tracker, but Fasting Tracker adds full fasting tracking through Garmin Connect IQ — including live phase display, countdown timers, streaks, and 7-day history — on any compatible Garmin watch.

What Garmin app tracks fasting?

Fasting Tracker is a Garmin Connect IQ widget for tracking intermittent fasting. It supports 16:8, 18:6, 20:4, OMAD, and any custom fasting target, with live phase progression and history directly on your Garmin watch.

Does it work for Garmin Forerunner, Fenix, and Venu?

Yes. Fasting Tracker is compatible with a wide range of Garmin watches, including Forerunner, Fenix, Epix, Venu, Vívoactive, Instinct, Enduro, and MARQ. Check the Connect IQ Store listing to confirm your exact model is supported.

Does Fasting Tracker work without a phone?

Yes. Once installed and configured, Fasting Tracker runs fully offline on your watch. Fasting data is stored locally on the device and never sent to a server or cloud.

Does it support OMAD, 18:6, and 20:4?

Yes. Fasting Tracker supports 16:8, 18:6, 20:4, OMAD, and any custom fasting duration you set. Choose a preset or enter your exact target fasting hours.

What fasting phases does it show?

Fasting Tracker shows seven estimated fasting phases based on elapsed time: Fed State (0h), Digesting (4h), Fat Burning (8h), Crossover (12h), Ketosis (14h), Autophagy (20h), and Peak Autophagy (24h+). These are informational estimates only.
