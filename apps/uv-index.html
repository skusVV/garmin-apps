UV Guard: Sun & Burn Forecast

Watch App Health Sun Safety

UV Guard is a sun safety app for compatible Garmin watches. Know when to put on sunscreen — before you regret it. Most UV apps live on your phone. This one lives on your wrist, where it matters — mid-run, on the slopes, or out on the water.

UV Guard shows your real-time UV index, a personalised burn countdown for your skin type, and the exact hours to avoid direct sun. No phone needed after data sync.

What you get

  • Current UV index with colour-coded risk level (Low → Extreme)
  • Burn time personalised to your skin type (Fitzpatrick scale I–VI)
  • SPF recommendation for current UV conditions
  • 24-hour UV forecast chart — plan your day around peak UV hours
  • Daily sun avoidance window — exactly when UV peaks and you should seek shade
  • Glance view — instant UV check without opening the app

UV Guard is provided for informational and educational purposes only and is not medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment. UV forecasts and burn time estimates are approximate and may vary based on individual skin sensitivity, cloud cover, reflective surfaces, and other environmental factors. Consult a qualified healthcare professional regarding safe sun exposure practices and skin health.

How to install UV Guard on your Garmin watch

  1. Open the Garmin Connect IQ Store listing for UV Guard
  2. Tap Try to start the 72-hour free trial
  3. In Garmin Connect → My Apps → UV Guard → Settings, select your Fitzpatrick skin type
  4. Sync your watch and open the app — UV data loads automatically

Frequently asked questions

How does UV Guard personalise burn time?

UV Guard uses the Fitzpatrick skin type scale (I–VI) to estimate how long your skin can safely be exposed to current UV levels before burning. You select your skin type once in settings. Type I (very fair) burns much faster than Type VI (deeply pigmented), and UV Guard adjusts the burn time estimate accordingly.

What is the Fitzpatrick skin type scale?

The Fitzpatrick scale is a classification system used in dermatology to describe human skin colour and its response to ultraviolet radiation. It ranges from Type I (very fair, always burns, never tans) to Type VI (deeply pigmented, never burns).

Does UV Guard work without a phone?

UV data is fetched during a phone sync. Once synced, the cached UV forecast for the day is available on your watch without requiring an active phone connection.

Are burn time estimates medically accurate?

Burn time estimates are based on standard UV index models and Fitzpatrick skin type classifications. They are approximate and individual results vary. UV Guard is for informational awareness only — not medical advice.
